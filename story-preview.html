She grabbed her backpack and headed downstairs where the smell of toast and her mom's humming filled the kitchen. But just as Mia reached the bottom step, something strange caught her eye through the window. There, sitting at the foot of the oak tree, was something she had never seen before in all her six years — something small, and glowing faintly gold, and watching her with enormous knowing eyes. Mia froze. Biscuit growled once, low in his throat, then went completely silent. And the thing at the tree — it smiled. "I've been waiting for you," it said. "There's something you need to know before you go to school today." Mia's heart hammered as she took one slow step toward the door, her hand reaching for the knob. Whatever was waiting in that yard, she had a feeling everything was about to change...
